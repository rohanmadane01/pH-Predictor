Acidic solution

pH below 7. Releases H⁺ ions. Sour taste. Examples: HCl, H₂SO₄, vinegar, lemon juice.

Neutral solution

pH equals 7. Balanced H⁺ and OH⁻ ions. Pure water at 25°C is the classic example.

Basic solution

pH above 7. Releases OH⁻ ions. Bitter and slippery. Examples: NaOH, NH₃, soap.

Formulas used
Strong acid

pH = −log[H⁺]

Weak acid · Ka = 1.8×10⁻⁵

pH = −log(√(Ka × C))

Strong base

pOH = −log[OH⁻] · pH = 14 − pOH

Weak base · Kb = 1.8×10⁻⁵
